What Makes A Quote Better

The more accurate the information, the better the starting quote.

Transmission and drivetrain pricing depends heavily on the vehicle, drivetrain layout, unit type, symptoms, internal condition, parts availability, converter needs, and whether the unit is still installed.

A detailed message makes it easier to determine whether the job is a good fit, whether it should be quoted as a bench build or installed job, and whether Basic, Standard, or Upgraded options make the most sense.

Vehicle

Year, make, model, engine, mileage, 2WD, 4WD, or AWD.

Symptoms

Slipping, no movement, harsh shifts, no reverse, leaks, noise, vibration, overheating, or codes.

Unit Status

Still in the vehicle, already removed, partially disassembled, or unknown.

Goal

Budget repair, daily driver reliability, towing, work truck, performance, or long-term ownership.

Contact Questions

Common questions before requesting a quote.

Is text or call better?

Text is usually fastest because you can send vehicle details, pictures, videos, symptoms, and unit information in one place.

Can I get a final price online?

A starting quote may be possible, but final pricing depends on inspection, vehicle configuration, internal condition, parts, and hard-part damage.

Do I need an appointment?

Yes. All work is handled by appointment so jobs can be reviewed, scheduled, and completed without rushed workflow.

Can I ask if my unit is supported?

Yes. Send your vehicle details and the transmission or drivetrain unit if known. Some units are accepted case-by-case.
