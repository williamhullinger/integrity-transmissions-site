Additional Drivetrain Work

Transmission work is only part of the drivetrain.

Transfer cases, differentials, bench builds, and supporting drivetrain components may also be accepted depending on the unit, vehicle, symptoms, and parts availability.

If the problem feels like grinding, binding, popping, whining, vibration, leaking, no 4WD engagement, or drivetrain noise, send the details before assuming it is only the transmission.

Important Fit Note

The listed unit is only the starting point.

The same transmission can need a very different repair depending on the vehicle, symptoms, mileage, prior work, internal damage, converter condition, cooler contamination, electronics, towing use, tire size, and customer expectations.

Some units are accepted quickly. Some need inspection first. Some are better declined if the scope does not make sense. That selectivity is part of keeping the quality high.

Send this information

  • Year, make, and model
  • Engine size
  • 2WD, 4WD, or AWD
  • Transmission or unit type if known
  • Current symptoms
  • Whether the unit is installed or removed
  • Any prior repair attempts

Supported Unit Questions

Common questions before sending your vehicle details.

Do you rebuild every transmission listed?

No. Listed units are supported or commonly requested, but final acceptance depends on application, condition, parts availability, and build scope.

What if my transmission is not listed?

Send the vehicle details anyway. This list is not complete, and additional units may be accepted case-by-case.

Why are some units case-by-case?

Some transmissions require programming, expensive hard parts, special tools, or inspection before accepting the job confidently.

Do you accept bench builds?

Yes. Supported transmissions can be brought in for bench rebuild work after the unit and scope are reviewed.
