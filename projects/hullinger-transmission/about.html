The Story

A hands-on drivetrain business built around standards.

Integrity Transmission & Drivetrain serves customers in the Springfield, Ozark, Rogersville, Nixa, Republic, and surrounding Southwest Missouri area with focused transmission and drivetrain work. The business was created for customers who want more than a quick parts swap or a vague repair estimate.

Transmission problems can be stressful because they are expensive, technical, and often misunderstood. A customer may know the vehicle is slipping, not moving, leaking, overheating, or making noise, but the real condition is usually found during inspection and teardown. That is why clear communication matters from the beginning.

The approach here is simple: screen the job first, explain the likely direction, inspect carefully, communicate what is found, and build the repair around the condition of the unit and the way the vehicle is used.

Quality Standards

What “done right” means here.

A transmission or drivetrain repair is more than getting the vehicle moving again. The work has to address the cause of failure, the condition of the hard parts, the cleanliness of the unit, and the supporting systems that can affect the repair after it leaves.

Inspection

Internal wear, hard-part damage, contamination, and common failure areas are evaluated before final repair direction.

Cleaning

Clean parts, clean assembly, and contamination control are critical for a repair that has a real chance of lasting.

Parts Decisions

Converters, bands, clutches, seals, bushings, valves, solenoids, and upgrades are considered based on the actual job.

Communication

Major findings, hard-part damage, and changes in scope should be discussed before moving forward.
